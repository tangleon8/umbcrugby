Physical

Train Your Body

Rugby demands strength, speed, and endurance. Here's how to prepare.

Strength Training

Build functional strength with squats, deadlifts, and box jumps. Focus on legs and core for explosive power on the field.

Cardiovascular Fitness

Combine traditional cardio with HIIT workouts. Rugby requires constant switching between sprinting, jogging, and recovery.

Nutrition & Hydration

Fuel your body with proteins, complex carbs, and plenty of fruits and vegetables. Stay hydrated before, during, and after training.

Rest & Recovery

Your muscles grow during rest, not training. Aim for 8 hours of sleep and take proper rest days to prevent injury and maximize gains.

Mental

Train Your Mind

Rugby is as much a mental game as it is physical. Learn to think like a rugby player.

Learn the Basics

Familiarize yourself with rules and terminology. Understanding the game reduces confusion and builds confidence on the field.

Watch Matches

Study games at all levels. Pay attention to positioning, strategies, and how players in your position make decisions.

Know the Laws

The official World Rugby laws evolve regularly. Stay updated to play within the latest standards and anticipate referee decisions.

Master Your Position

Each position has unique responsibilities. Focus on developing the specific skills that make your role impactful.
